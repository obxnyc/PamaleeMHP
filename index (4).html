💡 Items You May Be Excluding
Lot rent escalation clause — model assumes flat lot rent; even 3%/yr compounding materially changes 10-yr returns. Consider adding an annual bump toggle.
Vacancy ramp — the model fills all pads immediately. In reality pad-fill takes 6–18 months. A ramp schedule would show true cash-on-cash at stabilization vs. acquisition.
Eviction / legal costs — budget $500–1,500/event. At 49 pads with month-to-month tenants, plan for 2–3/year minimum.
Home turn costs — when a tenant vacates, expect $2,000–5,000 in cleaning, minor repairs, and re-marketing before the next placement.
Water/sewer master meter — if park is on master meter and sub-bills tenants, model the spread. If not, it's a direct park expense not captured here.
Loan origination fees — 1–2% of bank note at closing is a real cash cost that should reduce your net cash at close calculation.
Seller note balloon risk — at 3-yr balloon, model what the refinance looks like at current rates and whether DSCR survives a rate increase.
Dealer margin — your Larabee Homes LLC dealer margin on each home sale (difference between invoice and retail sale price) is a separate profit center not reflected in the operator model here.

Real Estate Professional Status — IRS Qualification Guide & Strategies
The Three Requirements — All Must Be Met (§469(c)(7))
1
750-Hour Requirement
You must spend more than 750 hours during the tax year performing services in real property trades or businesses in which you materially participate. This is approximately 14–15 hours per week. Hours must be documented contemporaneously — a calendar log, time-tracking app, or spreadsheet maintained throughout the year. Year-end reconstruction is a major audit risk and is routinely rejected by IRS examiners.
2
The 50% Test
More than 50% of your total personal services for the year must be in real property trades or businesses in which you materially participate. If a W-2 spouse works 2,000 hours, the managing spouse must log more than 1,000 hours in real estate to clear 50%. This is why the two-spouse structure is so common: one spouse earns W-2 income, the other manages the real estate business full-time and qualifies — and on a joint return, REP status applies to the entire MFJ return, offsetting the W-2 spouse's income with no cap.
3
Material Participation — Each Activity
You must materially participate in each rental activity. The IRS provides seven tests — the most commonly used: (a) 500+ hours in the activity, (b) substantially all participation is by you, or (c) 100+ hours and no other person participates more than you. Critical: make a grouping election under Reg. §1.469-9(g) to treat all rental properties as a single activity — without it, you must satisfy material participation on each property individually, which is dramatically harder to prove across a growing portfolio.
Documentation Checklist
✓ Daily time log: date, hours, specific activity description
✓ Calendar entries, emails, texts showing active management decisions
✓ Tenant communications, lease signings, inspection records
✓ Contractor coordination, repair authorizations, vendor invoices
✓ Mileage log for all property visits
✓ Grouping election statement attached to Form 1040 each year
✓ If spouse qualifies: separate hour log showing their personal service hours across all occupations to establish the 50% test
